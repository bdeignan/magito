# Federal Plain Language Guidelines

Follow these federal communication guidelines to focus on readability.

## Key Principles
- **Know the audience**: Write for the specific reader.
- **Use active voice**: State clearly who does what (e.g., "The user must approve the plan," not "The plan must be approved").
- **Everyday language**: Avoid legal, technical, or bureaucratic jargon.
- **Limit sentence length**: Target an average of 15–20 words per sentence.
- **Short paragraphs**: Limit each paragraph to one topic.
- **Lead with the main point**: Put the most important information first.
- **Use lists and tables**: Use bullet points and numbers to make complex steps easy to read.
