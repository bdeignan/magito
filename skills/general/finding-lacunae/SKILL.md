# Finding Lacunae

Find a *lacuna*: a missing keystone whose absence is explained by a specific
structural force. A keystone is an element the target needs to meet its own
goals or to occupy a defensible place among relevant alternatives. Do not call
an unsearched area, a personal preference, or an unsupported novelty a lacuna.

Research and diagnose only. Do not edit the target or turn a finding into a
product pitch. A valid conclusion is that no defensible lacuna was found.

## Workflow

1. **Set the frame.** State whether the target is a topic or artifact. Derive
   its apparent goals, boundaries, stakeholders, constraints, and quality
   dimensions. State assumptions, then continue unless the user corrects them.
   If the available material cannot support a usable frame, ask one focused
   question.
2. **Collect evidence.** Start with primary sources and authoritative
   documentation. Use weaker sources only when necessary, label their limits,
   and separate supplied material from external evidence. Record the search
   boundary: what was examined, where, and what may be missing.
3. **Map the relevant field.** Identify the main approaches, omissions,
   constraints, and trade-offs that bear on the frame. Map only enough to test
   a claim; do not produce a survey for its own sake.
4. **Form and test hypotheses.** Propose one or more possible axes or missing
   keystones. Treat every axis as a hypothesis, not a discovered fact. Check
   whether the apparent gap already exists, is unsupported by the search, is a
   false dichotomy, or follows from a constraint the target accepts.
5. **Name the keeping-empty force.** For each surviving candidate, identify a
   concrete mechanism that explains the absence: incentives, cost structure,
   regulation, technical limits, organizational incentives, an explicit design
   trade-off, or another evidenced constraint. Cite the support. **No specific force = boring gap.** Discard candidates that fail this test; never emit
   them as lacunae.
6. **Classify the evidence.** Give a calibrated read: likely undiscovered,
   deliberately excluded, already attempted and failed, or unresolved. Do not
   claim certainty when the evidence cannot distinguish these cases.
7. **Recommend a response.** Recommend a proportionate next action: investigate,
   specify, test, change the artifact, preserve the constraint, or make no
   change. Do not invent a fill for the gap.

## Output

Start with the stated frame and evidence coverage. Then give a ranked report
with only candidates that survived the force test:

| Rank | Named gap | Evidence and search limit | Keeping-empty force | Classification | Confidence | Recommended response |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|

For each finding, explain the reasoning from field map to force in a short
chain and cite the supporting sources. Label confidence as high, medium, or
low, based on both evidence quality and coverage. Include a final `No defensible lacuna found` result when every candidate fails the test.

## Boundary with `challenging-assumptions`

Use `challenging-assumptions` to critique a known plan, design, or change for
its internal assumptions and failure modes. Use this skill to research the
surrounding field and identify missing keystones. Use both only when the task
needs both an external gap diagnosis and an internal critique; keep their
findings separate.
